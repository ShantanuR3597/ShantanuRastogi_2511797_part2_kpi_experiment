# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

The company recently launched a new onboarding and activation campaign for new users of its subscription-based digital product. To evaluate the effectiveness of the new experience, users were divided into two groups: a Control group that experienced the existing onboarding process and a Treatment group that experienced the new onboarding campaign.

The primary business decision is whether the new onboarding experience should be launched to all users. This decision has a direct impact on user activation, conversion to paid plans, customer engagement, revenue generation, and the overall customer experience. It also affects multiple stakeholders, including the Product Team, Growth Team, Revenue Team, Customer Support Team, and company leadership.

The objective of this analysis is to determine whether the Treatment group demonstrates a meaningful improvement over the Control group in key business metrics. Particular attention will be given to user activation, onboarding completion, paid conversion, revenue performance, and customer engagement.

While improvements in conversion and revenue are important, the company must also evaluate potential risks before making a launch decision. These risks include increased refund requests, higher support ticket volumes, lower engagement levels, longer conversion times, or negative impacts on specific customer segments. A successful experiment should improve primary business outcomes without creating unacceptable trade-offs in these guardrail metrics.

To support a final recommendation, this analysis will evaluate experiment performance using a structured KPI framework, define a North Star Metric, perform statistical testing, compare business outcomes across Control and Treatment groups, assess guardrail metrics, and review segment-level performance. The final recommendation will be based on both statistical evidence and business impact.


## North Star Metric

The selected North Star Metric for this experiment is **Paid Conversion Rate**, defined as the percentage of users who convert from a new user to a paid customer.

**Formula:**

Paid Conversion Rate = Converted Users ÷ Total Users

This metric was selected because it directly reflects the business objective of increasing revenue-generating customers. The onboarding campaign is designed to improve user activation and guide users through the journey from signup to paid conversion. Therefore, any successful onboarding experience should ultimately result in a higher paid conversion rate.

Other metrics such as landing page visit rate, trial start rate, onboarding completion rate, and engagement score are important supporting metrics because they help explain user behavior throughout the conversion funnel. However, these metrics represent intermediate steps rather than the final business outcome.

Paid Conversion Rate also has a direct relationship with business growth because an increase in paying customers contributes to revenue generation and customer acquisition efficiency.

While optimizing for Paid Conversion Rate is important, focusing on it alone may create unintended consequences. A campaign could increase conversions while simultaneously increasing refund requests, support ticket volumes, or reducing overall customer satisfaction. Therefore, guardrail metrics such as Refund Rate, Support Ticket Rate, and Average Days to Convert must also be monitored before making a final launch recommendation.


## KPI Tree

The selected North Star Metric is Paid Conversion Rate, which represents the percentage of users who convert into paying customers. To understand the factors influencing this metric, a KPI Tree was developed to identify the key drivers of conversion performance.

The primary drivers include Trial Start Rate, Onboarding Completion Rate, User Engagement, and Revenue Outcomes. Trial Start Rate measures how effectively users move from the landing page to the trial experience. Onboarding Completion Rate measures how successfully users complete the activation journey. User Engagement is represented through the Engagement Score, which indicates product adoption and user activity. Revenue Outcomes are evaluated using Revenue 30D and Days to Convert.

In addition to growth metrics, guardrail metrics are included to ensure that improvements in conversion do not negatively affect customer experience. Refund Rate and Support Tickets 30D are monitored to identify potential issues that may arise from the new onboarding experience.

The KPI Tree provides a structured framework for evaluating experiment performance and understanding which factors contribute most significantly to paid conversion.
